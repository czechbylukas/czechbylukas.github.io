Practice Accusative (4th Case)

Training Guide: Mastering the Accusative (4. pád)

Active Recall: Direct Objects & Prepositions

The Accusative case is your primary tool for everyday actions. This practice session helps you master the "Who or What" of the sentence, specifically focusing on how feminine and animate masculine nouns change.

Restaurant Essentials:

Practicing Dám si... is the best way to learn the Accusative. Note how voda becomes vodu and rýže becomes rýži, while pivo stays the same.

People and Relationships:

When expressing feelings, the person being loved or liked moves into the 4th case. This is where you see the important -a ending for animate masculine nouns like Petra.

Optimization Strategy:

  • ✔ Step 1: Identify if the noun is feminine (usually ending in -u or -i).
  • ✔ Step 2: Watch for the Accusative prepositions like pro (for) and na (onto).
  • ✔ Step 3: Once you can recall the endings effortlessly, proceed to the 📝 Test.
