How to practice Czech Past Tense Grammar

The Czech past tense (minulý čas) is constructed from two primary components: the auxiliary verb (jsem, jsi, jsme, jste) and the past participle (the verb form ending in -l, -la, -lo, -li, -ly). This tool automates the construction of these sentences.

  1. ✔ Select Gender: This is critical for determining the correct -l vs -la endings for the past participle.
  2. ✔ Enter Verb: Input a Czech infinitive (e.g., dělat) or click the randomizer 🎲.
  3. ✔ Step-by-Step Build: Build the sentence from subject to final participle, hearing native audio feedback at each stage.
