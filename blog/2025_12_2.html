St. Nicholas, the Devil, and the Angel

In the Czech Republic, we celebrate St. Nicholas on December 5th.

St. Nicholas walks through the streets in the evening and visits children at home.

He is accompanied by an Angel, who praises the children, and a Devil, who scares them a little.

Good children usually say a poem or a song and receive sweets, fruit, or small gifts.

Children who misbehaved get coal or a potato, but usually only as a joke.

It is a popular evening full of fun and a little bit of fear.

The Tradition of St. Nicholas, the Devil, and the Angel in the Czech Republic

Every year on December 5th, a trio of characters: St. Nicholas, the Devil, and the Angel, walks through Czech towns and villages.

This tradition has a long history and is connected to Saint Nicholas, who was known for his kindness and generosity toward the poor.

St. Nicholas represents goodness and fairness, the Angel symbolizes protection and purity, and the Devil serves as a reminder of the consequences of bad behavior.

Children often prepare for the visit by learning poems or songs, hoping to earn sweets, fruit, or small gifts.

Even though the Devil can look scary, most children know it is only part of the fun.

The St. Nicholas evening brings families together and adds excitement, joy, and a bit of Christmas magic.
