What is Advent?

Advent is a four-week period before Christmas.

The first Advent Sunday signals the start of Advent.

Every Sunday we light one candle on the Advent wreath.

The Advent wreath has four candles and sometimes a fifth candle in the middle.

Families visit Christmas markets and buy decorations.

People prepare their homes, bake cookies, and sing carols.

Advent and traditions in the Czech Republic

Advent refers to the four weeks before Christmas and serves as a time to prepare for the Christmas.

The first Advent Sunday begins a cycle of four Sundays when each week another candle on the wreath is lit.

The Advent wreath symbolizes hope, peace, joy, and love; the candles remind people of the approaching Christmas celebration.

During Advent, people often visit Christmas markets, buy gifts, and bake traditional pastries such as vanilla crescents and gingerbread.

Some also prefer a quieter pace, attend Advent concerts, or go to early morning masses.

Advent combines religious and folk customs that bring families together and create a Christmas atmosphere.
