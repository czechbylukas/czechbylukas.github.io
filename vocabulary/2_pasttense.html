Study Guide: Czech Past Tense & Verbs

Mastering Past Tense Daily Activities

This lesson introduces high-frequency Czech verbs and time expressions. Learning to say what you did včera (yesterday) or o víkendu (at the weekend) is a major milestone in beginner Czech proficiency.

Common Activity Verbs:

Focus on essential actions like pracovat (to work), spát (to sleep), and vařit (to cook). These form the basis of most "small talk" in Czechia.

Time Expressions:

Learn to anchor your sentences using v pondělí (on Monday) or v noci (at night). Note how Czech sentences often place the verb at the start or middle depending on emphasis.
